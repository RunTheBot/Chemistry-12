\section*{Introduction}

This lab explores the pH of salt solutions, the acid dissociation of weak acids, and the properties of buffer solutions. When a neutralization occurs, a salt is formed. Neutral salts form when a strong acid and a strong base react. A non-neutral salt forms when a weak acid or a weak base is involved, it exhibits either acidic or basic properties due to hydrolysis reactions in water. 

Weak acids/bases only partially dissociate in aqueous solution. The strength of a weak acid or bases is quantified by its acid dissociation constant, $K_a$ or $K_b$ respectively. By measuring the pH of buffer solutions containing a weak acid/weak base and its conjugate base/acid, we can determine the $K_a$/$K_b$ value. A buffer solution resists changes in pH when small amounts of acid or base are added, making it useful in many chemical and biological applications.

This lab consists of three parts. First, the pH of different salt solutions will be measured using a pH probe. Second, buffer solutions will be prepared by combining a weak acid with its conjugate base, and their pH will be recorded to calculate Ka values. Finally, the buffer's ability to resist pH changes will be tested by adding a strong base and comparing the pH change to that of pure water treated the same way.

\textbf{Safety considerations:} Wear gloves at all times during this experiment, as some solutions may cause skin irritation upon direct contact.


\section*{Procedure}

\subsection*{Part 1: pH of Salt Solutions}

\begin{procedureflowchart}
    % Step 1: NaCl
    \lstep{Beaker (100 mL)}
    \rstep{Clean 100 mL beaker was prepared}
    
    \lstep{Graduated Cylinder}
    \rstep{20.0 mL of 0.100 M NaCl solution was measured and added to beaker}
    
    \lstep{pH Probe}
    \rstep{pH probe was calibrated according to manufacturer instructions}
    \rstep{pH of NaCl solution was measured and recorded}
    
    \lstep{Deionized Water}
    \rstep{pH probe was rinsed thoroughly with deionized water}
    
    % Steps 2 & 3: Na2CO3 and NaHSO3
    \rstep{Procedure was repeated for 0.100 M Na$_2$CO$_3$ solution}
    \rstep{Procedure was repeated for 0.100 M NaHSO$_3$ solution}
    \rstep{pH probe was rinsed after each measurement}
\end{procedureflowchart}

\subsection*{Part 2: Ka of a Weak Acid}

\begin{procedureflowchart}
    % Step 1: Buffer preparation (HSO3-)
    \lstep{Beaker (250 mL)}
    \rstep{Clean 250 mL beaker was prepared}
    
    \lstep{Graduated Cylinder}
    \rstep{20.0 mL of 0.1 M NaHSO$_3$ solution was measured and added to beaker}
    
    \rstep{20.0 mL of 0.100 M Na$_2$SO$_3$ solution was measured and added to beaker}
    
    \lstep{Glass Rod}
    \rstep{Solution was stirred thoroughly to mix}
    
    \lstep{pH Probe}
    \rstep{pH of buffer solution was measured and recorded}
    
    \lstep{Deionized Water}
    \rstep{pH probe was rinsed thoroughly}
    
    % Step 2: Buffer preparation (CH3COO-)
    \rstep{Procedure was repeated using CH$_3$COOH and NaCH$_3$COO solutions}
    \rstep{pH of second buffer solution was measured and recorded}
\end{procedureflowchart}

\subsection*{Part 3: Buffer Solution Testing}

\begin{procedureflowchart}
    % Step 1: Buffer + NaOH
    \lstep{Buffer Solution (from Part 2)}
    \rstep{40.0 mL of NaHSO$_3$/Na$_2$SO$_3$ buffer solution from Part 2 was prepared}
    
    \lstep{Graduated Cylinder}
    \rstep{10.0 mL of NaOH solution was measured and added to buffer}
    
    \lstep{pH Probe}
    \rstep{Resulting pH was measured and recorded}
    
    % Step 2: Water + NaOH (control)
    \lstep{Beaker}
    \rstep{40 mL of deionized water was measured and added to clean beaker}
    
    \lstep{Graduated Cylinder}
    \rstep{10.0 mL of NaOH solution was measured and added to water}
    
    \lstep{pH Probe}
    \rstep{Resulting pH was measured and recorded}
\end{procedureflowchart}
