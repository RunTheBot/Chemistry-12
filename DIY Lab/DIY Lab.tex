\section*{Purpose}
To compare the measured voltage of non-standard vinegar cells using copper as the inert cathode with the theoretical cell potential predicted by standard electrode potentials referenced to the SHE.

\section*{Background}
The experimental cell uses an inert copper strip as the cathode surface, while the actual reduction is hydrogen ion reduction:
\begin{align*}
\text{Zn}_{(s)} &\rightarrow \text{Zn}^{2+}_{(aq)} + 2\text{e}^- \\
2\text{H}^+_{(aq)} + 2\text{e}^- &\rightarrow \text{H}_{2(g)}
\end{align*}

The standard hydrogen electrode (SHE) potential is defined as 0.00 V. Using standard reduction potentials gives the theoretical reference values:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Zn/Zn$^{2+}$ vs SHE: $E^\circ_{cell} = +0.76\,\text{V}$
  \item Al/Al$^{3+}$ vs SHE: $E^\circ_{cell} = +1.66\,\text{V}$
\end{itemize}

Because vinegar is a weak acetic acid solution, the actual hydrogen ion concentration is much lower than 1.0 M. This means the measured voltage will be lower than the standard SHE-based values, and the Nernst equation can be used to explain the difference.

The Nernst equation is written as:
\[
E_{cell} = E^\circ_{cell} - \frac{0.0592\,\text{V}}{n} \log(Q)
\]

\section*{Materials}
\begin{itemize}
  \item 100 mL beaker
  \item Copper strip (electrode)
  \item Zinc strip (electrode)
  \item White vinegar (acetic acid solution)
  \item Multimeter with leads
  \item Alligator clips
  \item Distilled water (for rinsing)
  \item Sandpaper or steel wool (for cleaning metal strips)
  \item Safety goggles and gloves
\end{itemize}

\section*{Procedure Flowchart}
\begin{procedureflowchart}
    \lstep{Clean Strips}
    \rstep{Remove surface oxides from all metal strips using sandpaper or steel wool}
    \lstep{Rinse \& Dry}
    \rstep{Rinse each strip with distilled water and gently dry with a paper towel}
    \lstep{Prepare Electrolyte}
    \rstep{Pour approximately 50 mL of vinegar into the beaker}
    \lstep{Insert Copper Cathode}
    \rstep{Place the copper strip into the vinegar as the inert cathode}
    \lstep{Insert First Anode}
    \rstep{Place the zinc strip into the vinegar without touching the copper strip}
    \lstep{Connect Voltmeter}
    \rstep{Attach copper to the positive lead and zinc to the negative lead}
    \lstep{Measure Zn Cell Voltage}
    \rstep{Record the voltage for the copper/zinc vinegar cell}
    \lstep{Switch Metal}
    \rstep{Remove zinc and insert the aluminum strip in the same position}
    \lstep{Measure Al Cell Voltage}
    \rstep{Record the voltage for the copper/aluminum vinegar cell}
    \lstep{Observe Cell}
    \rstep{Note hydrogen bubble formation and electrode appearance}
    \lstep{Cleanup}
    \rstep{Rinse electrodes, turn off the multimeter, and dispose of vinegar safely}
\end{procedureflowchart}
